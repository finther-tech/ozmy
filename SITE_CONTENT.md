### SLO vs SLI Section
**Section Title:** SLO vs SLI

**Description:** Understanding the building blocks of service level management

#### SLI Card (Service Level Indicator)

**Definition:** A carefully defined quantitative measure of some aspect of the level of service that is provided.

**Examples:**
- Request latency
- Error rate
- Throughput
- Availability (uptime)

#### SLO Card (Service Level Objective)

**Definition:** A target value or range of values for a service level that is measured by an SLI.

**Examples:**
- 99.9% availability monthly
- p95 latency < 200ms
- Error rate < 0.1%
- 1000 requests/second

### Best Practices Section
**Section Title:** Observability Best Practices

**Description:** Key principles for effective monitoring

**Four Best Practices:**

1. **Start with User Experience**
   - Measure what matters to your users, not just what's easy to measure.

2. **Define Clear SLOs**
   - Establish measurable objectives that align with business goals.

3. **Use Meaningful SLIs**
   - Choose indicators that accurately reflect service performance and reliability.

4. **Monitor Continuously**
   - Maintain real-time visibility to detect and respond to issues quickly.
